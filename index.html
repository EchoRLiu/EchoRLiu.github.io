Yuhong E. Liu

Hello! I am currently a Theoretical Neuroscience Ph.D. student at University of Bonn, working with Prof. Tatjana Tchumatchenko, with affiliation at University of Bonn Medical Center.

My current research projects focus on the application of computational modeling to understand the progression of neurodegenerative diseases, with an emphasis on protein dynamics. I collaborate closely with Prof. Sybille Krauss, Prof. Michael Schmeisser, and Prof. Susann Schweiger.

I earned my M.S. from Department of Applied Mathematics at University of Washington in 2021. I worked with Prof. Douglas Martin on robust Garch process modeling methods and Prof. Juris Vagners in Autonomous Flight Systems Laboratory (now Control & Trustworthy Robotics Lab), on applying computer vision to unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV) for search and rescue missions.

You can know more about my current and past research in the research section.
